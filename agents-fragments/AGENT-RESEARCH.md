# Research Profile

Use only for user-owned research repositories or when the user explicitly requests this personal research profile.

Use this module for research, paper-reading, academic writing, experiment design, and knowledge-base work.

## User Context

The user is a PhD student. Use high-standard academic and technical discussion; do not over-explain foundational material unless the user asks for it or the argument depends on it.

## Research Axes

1. **Neural data methods and mechanisms**: Develop algorithms, models, or frameworks for interpreting biological neural activity during cognitive functions, with emphasis on mechanism discovery, theory, and method-driven analysis.
2. **Neuroscience of higher human cognition**: Study language, mathematics, physical intuition, and related high-level cognition through psychophysics, cognitive neuroscience experiments, neuron-resolution human neural data, signal processing, and interpretation.
3. **Advanced mathematical methods for data science**: Apply topological data analysis, information geometry, functional analysis, dynamical systems, and related mathematical structures to data science and neural data analysis.
4. **Mathematical foundations of machine learning**: Understand generative models, optimization theory, learning dynamics, and other core ML problems from a principled mathematical perspective, not only phenomenological intuition.

## Intellectual Anchors

Relevant background includes neural dynamics and recurrent-network theory (Ostojic, Sussillo, Fusi), probabilistic neural-data models (Sahani, Linderman), human higher cognition (Dehaene, Sablé-Meyer), computational cognitive science (Tenenbaum, Gershman), and systems neuroscience experiments (Liping Wang). Core questions include how biological neural activity implements higher cognition, latent structure in neural population activity, and the neural basis of language, mathematics, and physical intuition.

## Research Workflow Preferences

- Prefer selective, high-quality paper reading over broad shallow coverage.
- Favor pre-understanding, triage, and then targeted close reading.
- Obsidian is the default knowledge base; `.md` and `.html` are both acceptable informal research artifacts.
- LaTeX is preferred for formal technical writing; Word is acceptable for document workflows that require it.
- The user is comfortable with high AI autonomy: produce the artifact directly when requirements are clear, and leave human review to validation.

## Output Preferences

- Write dense, high-signal research notes rather than flat summaries.
- Preserve uncertainty, source grounding, and evidence boundaries.
- Use LaTeX for mathematical structure when relevant.
- Use figures, callouts, side comments, tables, and structured sections when they improve comprehension.
- HTML is often preferable to plain Markdown for deep reading artifacts; Markdown is preferable for durable Obsidian notes.
- Chinese and English are both acceptable; keep technical terms in English when that is the clearer disciplinary convention.
